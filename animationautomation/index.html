The Pipeline

Claude does ninety percent
of the production.

Not the prompting. Not one step. The production. From the reference sheets that hold your character together, to the script, to every clip, to the voice, the music, the edit, the captions — and out the door to the platforms. Read that list again slowly. That is the whole job.

01
It builds the cast
Character & scene reference sheets

Claude interviews you, then generates the character sheet — five angles, one face — and the scene sheets that lock the world she lives in. Every asset gets logged and reused, so shot forty still looks like shot one. This is where consistency stops being luck and becomes a filing system.

02
It writes the film
Idea scoring · script · storyboard

It scores your idea out of twenty and tells you honestly when it's a twelve — then offers three better ones. It writes the beats, cuts every line to fifteen words, and builds the locked-world table that stops the model redecorating your kitchen between shots.

03
It shoots it
Seedance 2.0 · stills → clips, in parallel

Stills first — cheap to iterate. Then each approved still becomes the first frame of its clip, generated on Seedance 2.0 — the newest and most powerful video model available today. Native audio, real performance, and the fidelity that makes people forget to ask whether it's AI. That first-frame anchor is precisely what stops your character morphing mid-reel. Claude fires the generations in parallel, polls them, retries the failures, and files everything where it belongs.

04
It gives her a voice
Cloned voice · Suno score

A cloned voice — hers, or yours — carrying the accent and the cadence, because get the voice wrong and the comments will roast you. Then an original Suno track scored to the mood of the cut. No library, no licensing, no copyright headache.

05
It cuts it
Assembly · captions · export

Crossfades, audio blends, burned-in captions positioned above the Instagram UI zone, exported to spec. You never open an editor. If the caption timing is off, you say so, and it re-renders in seconds.

06
It ships it
Posting · AI label · performance log

Out to the platforms, AI-labelled — which is required, and also the best content angle you have. Forty-eight hours later it logs the views, the shares and the comments people actually quoted. Those quotes are next week's content.

So what's your ten percent? Taste.

Deciding who she is. Knowing which take is the one. Saying no at the gates. The machine will happily produce a hundred beautiful, forgettable reels — judgement is the part it can't do for you, and it's the part this class actually teaches.

Gate One
The script

Not a frame is generated until you approve the storyboard. Ideas are free. Video is not.

Gate Two
The stills

Every frame checked before it moves: does the face match the sheet? The room? Is the prop the right size?

Gate Three
The performance

Face, scene, realism, acting. All four, or it goes back. You don't ship "close enough" at 1am.
