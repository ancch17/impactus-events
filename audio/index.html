What you'll walk out able to do.

Hour 01

Turn a sentence into a finished song

Type one line about the song in your head, and Suno writes and performs it — melody, vocals, production, all of it. Within the first hour you'll have complete songs that are genuinely yours, plus the prompt patterns that make them sound intended, not generated. No theory. No instruments.

Your first song
Hour 02

Make it unmistakably yours

Rework a lucky draft like a producer — extend it, rewrite a verse, swap the style mid-song. Then the moves nobody expects from a beginner: hum or sing a fragment and let Suno finish the rest, put your own singing voice into the track, and save your sound as a Persona — one consistent AI artist that could carry a whole EP.

Your voice · Personas
Hour 03

Finish it so it sounds finished

The difference between "AI demo" and "my song" is the finishing. Generate lifelike sound effects in ElevenLabs — birdsong, rain, a crowd — and blend everything into one clean, polished mix in Audacity. You leave with tracks that are done, not almost-done.

TRK 04

Before you ask.

Do I need any music background?

None at all — not a note. If you can describe the song you want in a sentence, you can make it. The class is built for complete beginners and hobbyist creators; we start from zero and you build up live.

What do I need to bring?

A laptop, headphones, a Suno account (see the next question about going paid), a free ElevenLabs account, and the free Audacity app. Set-up instructions are sent after you register.

Do I need the paid version of Suno?

Strongly recommended. This is a hands-on class, and the free tier's limits mean you'd largely be listening in rather than building. A paid plan lets you make every song yourself live — and unlocks the best parts of hour two: putting your own singing voice into a track, hum-and-extend, and Personas.

Is this live or recorded?

Live on Zoom, 7–10pm Singapore time. It's hands-on — you'll be making tracks alongside the class, with time for questions as you go.

What's the difference between the two dates?

Nothing — it's the same class on two evenings. Choose Wednesday 29 July or Thursday 30 July, whichever fits your week.

What will I walk away with?

Finished songs you wrote and produced yourself — plus custom sound effects, a clean final mix, and a repeatable workflow you can use again the very next morning. And if you're ever curious about releasing your music, you'll know exactly how that door opens (DistroKid, streaming royalties) — with zero pressure to walk through it.
